# PART 1: FOUNDATION - UNDERSTANDING NEO-SOUL HARMONY {#part-1-foundation}

## 1.1 What Defines Neo-Soul Harmony?

Neo-soul emerged in the 1990s, blending:
- Classic soul and R&B chord progressions
- Jazz extended harmony
- Hip-hop rhythmic sensibilities
- Gospel voice leading traditions

**Key Characteristics:**
- Extended chords (9ths, 11ths, 13ths)
- Chromatic voice leading
- Minor 7th chord movements
- Suspended and altered dominants
- Rich, warm voicings in middle register

## 1.2 The Neo-Soul Sound Palette

| Element | Traditional Soul | Neo-Soul |
|---------|-----------------|----------|
| Chord Types | Triads, 7ths | 9ths, 11ths, 13ths, altered |
| Voice Leading | Diatonic | Chromatic, parallel |
| Rhythm | On-beat emphasis | Syncopated, behind the beat |
| Texture | Full strums | Broken chords, arpeggios |
| Tone | Clean/slightly driven | Warm, compressed, chorus |

## 1.3 Essential Music Theory for Neo-Soul

### Intervals You Must Know

```
Minor 2nd (m2): 1 half-step - tension, dissonance
Major 2nd (M2): 2 half-steps - suspension sound
Minor 3rd (m3): 3 half-steps - sad, minor quality
Major 3rd (M3): 4 half-steps - happy, major quality
Perfect 4th (P4): 5 half-steps - suspended, open
Tritone (TT): 6 half-steps - unstable, wants to resolve
Perfect 5th (P5): 7 half-steps - stable, strong
Minor 6th (m6): 8 half-steps - tension
Major 6th (M6): 9 half-steps - sweet, nostalgic
Minor 7th (m7): 10 half-steps - bluesy, unresolved
Major 7th (M7): 11 half-steps - jazzy, sophisticated
```

### Chord Construction

**From Root C:**

```
Cmaj7:    C - E - G - B      (1 - 3 - 5 - 7)
Cmaj9:    C - E - G - B - D  (1 - 3 - 5 - 7 - 9)
Cmaj13:   C - E - G - B - A  (1 - 3 - 5 - 7 - 13) *omit 9 and 11
C6/9:     C - E - G - A - D  (1 - 3 - 5 - 6 - 9)

Cm7:      C - Eb - G - Bb    (1 - b3 - 5 - b7)
Cm9:      C - Eb - G - Bb - D (1 - b3 - 5 - b7 - 9)
Cm11:     C - Eb - G - Bb - F (1 - b3 - 5 - b7 - 11)

C7:       C - E - G - Bb     (1 - 3 - 5 - b7)
C9:       C - E - G - Bb - D (1 - 3 - 5 - b7 - 9)
C7#9:     C - E - G - Bb - D# (1 - 3 - 5 - b7 - #9) "Hendrix chord"
C7b9:     C - E - G - Bb - Db (1 - 3 - 5 - b7 - b9)
C7#5:     C - E - G# - Bb    (1 - 3 - #5 - b7)
C7alt:    C - E - G# - Bb - Db - D# (1 - 3 - #5 - b7 - b9 - #9)
```

# PART 3: PASSING CHORDS MASTERY {#part-3-passing-chords}

## 3.1 Chromatic Approach Chords

**Principle:** Approach any target chord from a half-step above or below, using the same chord quality.

### Ascending Approaches (from below)

```
Target: Dm7
Approach: Dbm7 → Dm7

Dbm7                 Dm7
e|---x---|          e|---x---|
B|---4---|    →     B|---5---|
G|---4---|          G|---5---|
D|---4---|          D|---5---|
A|---4---|          A|---5---|
E|---x---|          E|---x---|
```

### Descending Approaches (from above)

```
Target: Cmaj7
Approach: Dbmaj7 → Cmaj7

Dbmaj7               Cmaj7
e|---x---|          e|---x---|
B|---6---|    →     B|---5---|
G|---5---|          G|---4---|
D|---6---|          D|---5---|
A|---4---|          A|---3---|
E|---x---|          E|---x---|
```

### Exercise: Chromatic Approaches in C Major

```
| Cmaj7  Dbmaj7 | Dm7  Ebm7 | Em7  Fm7 | Fmaj7 F#m7 | Gmaj7 |
```

Play each chord for 2 beats, feeling the half-step resolution.

## 3.2 Chromatic Minor 7th Lines

**Principle:** Move through a series of minor 7th chords chromatically, regardless of key.

### Descending Minor 7th Line

```
Em7 → Ebm7 → Dm7 → Dbm7 → Cmaj7

Position on strings 5-4-3-2:

Em7:    x-7-5-7-8-x
Ebm7:   x-6-4-6-7-x
Dm7:    x-5-3-5-6-x
Dbm7:   x-4-2-4-5-x
Cmaj7:  x-3-2-4-5-x
```

### Ascending Minor 7th Line

```
Am7 → Bbm7 → Bm7 → Cm7 → Dbm7 → Dm7

Position on strings 5-4-3-2:

Am7:    x-0-2-0-1-x
Bbm7:   x-1-3-1-2-x
Bm7:    x-2-4-2-3-x
Cm7:    x-3-5-3-4-x
Dbm7:   x-4-6-4-5-x
Dm7:    x-5-7-5-6-x
```

### The "Soul Descension" - Classic Neo-Soul Movement

```
| Fmaj7 | Em7 Ebm7 | Dm7 | Dbmaj7 Cmaj7 |
```

This creates stepwise bass motion: F - E - Eb - D - Db - C

## 3.3 Diminished 7th Passing Chords

**Principle:** Diminished 7th chords can connect any two chords a whole step apart, or lead chromatically to the next chord.

### Function as Leading Tones

```
C#dim7 resolves to Dm (C# leads to D)
D#dim7 resolves to Em (D# leads to E)
F#dim7 resolves to G (F# leads to G)
G#dim7 resolves to Am (G# leads to A)
```

### Exercise: Diatonic Chords with Diminished Connectors in C

```
| Cmaj7 C#dim7 | Dm7 D#dim7 | Em7 | Fmaj7 F#dim7 | G7 G#dim7 | Am7 | Bm7b5 | Cmaj7 |
```

### Diminished Chord Shapes (All Moveable)

```
Shape 1 (Root on 6th string):
x-x-1-2-1-2 = Cdim7

Shape 2 (Root on 5th string):
x-3-4-2-4-x = Cdim7

Shape 3 (Root on 4th string):
x-x-x-2-4-2 = Cdim7
```

Remember: Diminished 7ths are symmetrical. The same shape repeats every 3 frets.

## 3.5 Tritone Substitutions

**Principle:** Any dominant 7th chord can be replaced by the dominant 7th a tritone away (6 half-steps).

### Common Tritone Subs

| Original | Tritone Sub |
|----------|-------------|
| G7 → C | Db7 → C |
| D7 → G | Ab7 → G |
| A7 → D | Eb7 → D |
| E7 → A | Bb7 → A |

### Why It Works

G7 = G - B - D - F
Db7 = Db - F - Ab - Cb(B)

They share the same tritone interval (B-F / F-B), which is what creates the pull to resolve.

### Application

```
Original:  | Cmaj7 | Am7 | Dm7 | G7  | Cmaj7 |
Tritone:   | Cmaj7 | Am7 | Dm7 | Db7 | Cmaj7 |
```

The bass moves: C - A - D - Db - C (chromatic approach to home!)

### Combined: Secondary Dominant + Tritone Sub

```
| Cmaj7 | Bb7 | Am7 | Ab7 | Gm7 | Gb7 | Fmaj7 | E7 | Am7 |
```

Each tritone sub leads chromatically to the next chord.

## 3.6 Passing Chord Combinations

### The "Gospel Walkdown"

```
| C | C/B | Am | Am/G | F | C/E | Dm | G |
```

Slash chords create the walking bass: C - B - A - G - F - E - D - G

### The "Neo-Soul Turnaround"

```
| Imaj7 | V7/vi | vi7 | V7/V | IVmaj7 | bVII7 | Imaj7 |

In C:
| Cmaj7 | E7#9 | Am9 | D9 | Fmaj9 | Bb13 | Cmaj9 |
```

### The "Chromatic Plunge"

```
| Cmaj7 | Bm7 Bb7 | Am7 Ab7 | Gm7 Gb7 | Fmaj7 |
```

Alternating m7 and dom7 chords descending chromatically.

### The Dorian Mode (Essential for Minor Neo-Soul)

```

```

**Why Dorian:** The raised 6th (F# in A Dorian) creates a sweeter minor sound than natural minor. This is the go-to minor mode for neo-soul.

### The Mixolydian Mode

```
G Mixolydian: G - A - B - C - D - E - F

Pattern 1:
e|--------------------3--5--7--|
B|-----------------3--5--7-----|
G|--------------4--5-----------|
D|----------3--5---------------|
A|-------3--5------------------|
E|-3--5--7---------------------|
```

**Why Mixolydian:** Used over dominant 7th chords. The b7 gives it that bluesy, gospel sound.

### The Major Bebop Scale

```
C Major Bebop: C - D - E - F - G - G# - A - B

This adds a chromatic passing tone (G#) between G and A.
Useful for creating smooth chromatic lines.
```

### The Minor Bebop Scale

```
A Minor Bebop: A - B - C - D - E - F - G - G#

Adds G# between G and A, connecting to the root.
```

## 4.2 Hybrid Scales for Neo-Soul

### The "Soul Scale" (Dorian + Blues Notes)

```

Combines Dorian with the blue note (D#/Eb).

Pattern:
e|----------------------5--7--8--|
B|-------------------5--7--8-----|
G|---------------4--5--6--7------|

```
G Gospel: G - A - B - C - D - E - F with emphasis on G - A - B - D - E

Play Mixolydian but emphasize pentatonic tones for that churchy sound.
```

## 4.4 Connecting Scales to Chords

### Chord-Scale Relationships

| Chord Type | Scale/Mode |
|------------|------------|
| Maj7 | Ionian, Lydian |
| m7 | Dorian, Aeolian |
| 7 (dominant) | Mixolydian, Blues |
| m7b5 | Locrian, Locrian #2 |
| dim7 | Diminished (whole-half) |
| 7alt | Altered (melodic minor 7th mode) |

### Targeting Chord Tones

**Rule:** Resolve your lines to chord tones on strong beats.

For Cmaj7 (C-E-G-B):
- Land on C, E, G, or B on beats 1 and 3
- Use other scale tones as passing notes

# PART 5: LEAD TECHNIQUES {#part-5-lead-techniques}

## 5.1 Slides

### Grace Note Slides (Quick Approach)

```
Slide into a note from a half-step or whole-step below:

    sl.
e|--/5---|  (Slide quickly from 4 to 5)
```

### Measured Slides

```
Count the slide as part of your rhythm:

    sl.........
e|--5-------7---|  (Slide takes a beat to arrive)
```

### Double-Stop Slides

```
Slide two notes together:

    sl.
B|--/5---|
G|--/4---|
```

## 5.2 Hammer-Ons and Pull-Offs

### Basic Legato

```
Hammer-on (ascending):
e|--5h7---|  (Pick 5, hammer to 7)

Pull-off (descending):
e|--7p5---|  (Pick 7, pull off to 5)
```

```
Rapid alternation:
e|--5h7p5h7p5h7---|
```

```

# PART 7: APPLICATION TO MAJOR TIZITA {#part-7-tizita}

## 7.1 Understanding Major Tizita

### The Scale

```
Major Tizita: 1 - 2 - 3 - 5 - 6

In C: C - D - E - G - A
In G: G - A - B - D - E
In D: D - E - F# - A - B
```

### Guitar Pattern (Key of C, Open Position)

```
e|---0--2-----------|  (G - A)
B|---0--3-----------|  (E - G)
G|---0--2-----------|  (C - D)
D|---0--2-----------|  (A - C)  Note: A is in scale, C is too
A|---0--3-----------|  (A - C)  
E|-------------------|
```

### Pattern 1 (Root on 6th String, Key of C, 8th Position)

```
e|-------8--10--|  (C - D)
B|----8--10-----|  (E - G)
G|----9--10-----|  (A)
D|-7--10--------|  (G)
A|-8--10--------|  (C)
E|-8--10--------|  (C)
```

## 7.2 Diatonic Chords in Major Tizita

Building chords from C-D-E-G-A only:

| Degree | Most Common Chord | Notes |
|--------|-------------------|-------|
| I | C, C6, C6/9 | C-E-G, C-E-G-A |
| II | Dsus2, Dsus4 | D-E-A, D-G-A |
| III | Em, Em7 (no 5) | E-G, E-G-A-D |
| V | G, G6 | G-D, G-D-E |
| VI | Am, Am7 (sus) | A-C-E, A-C-E-G |

### Most Used Progressions

**Progression 1:**
```
| C | Am | G | C |
```

**Progression 2:**
```
| C | G | Am | Em | C |
```

**Progression 3 (with 6th chords):**
```
| C6/9 | Am7 | G6 | C6/9 |
```

## 7.3 Adding Neo-Soul Passing Chords to Tizita

### Step 1: Identify the "Gaps"

In the progression | C | Am | G | C |:
- C to Am = major 3rd interval
- Am to G = whole step
- G to C = perfect 4th

### Step 2: Fill the Gaps

**Using Chromatic Approach:**
```
| C    Bbmaj7 | Am    Abmaj7 | G    Db7 | C |
```

**Using Diminished:**
```
| C    C#dim7 | Dm7   D#dim7 | Em7   | Am7   G#dim7 | G   | C |
```

**Using Secondary Dominants:**
```
| C    E7 | Am    D7 | G    G7 | C |
```

**Using Chromatic m7:**
```
| C | Bm7   Bbm7 | Am7 | Abm7   Gm7   Gbm7 | Fmaj7 | G7 | C |
```

### Step 3: Complete Neo-Soul Tizita Progression

**The Full Treatment:**

```
Bar 1:  | Cmaj9           |
Bar 2:  | Bm7    Bbmaj7   |
Bar 3:  | Am9             |
Bar 4:  | Abmaj7   D7#9   |
Bar 5:  | Gmaj7           |
Bar 6:  | Gbmaj7   Fmaj7  |
Bar 7:  | Em7     Eb7     |
Bar 8:  | Dm9     Db7     |
Bar 9:  | Cmaj9           |
```

## 7.4 Voice Leading in Tizita Context

### Keeping the Tizita Melody Clear

**Rule:** Your top note should outline the pentatonic melody while inner voices move chromatically.

```
Melody: C - D - E - G - A - G - E - D - C

Chord:  C   D   E   G   A   G   E   D   C  (melody on top)
        Cmaj7→Dm7→Em7→Gmaj7→Am7→Gmaj7→Em7→Dm7→Cmaj7

But we can substitute:
        Cmaj7→Dbmaj7→Em7→Gbmaj7→Am7→Abmaj7→Em7→Ebmaj7→Cmaj7
```

### Common Tizita Bass Lines

**Simple:**
```
| C     | A     | G     | C     |
```

**With Passing Tones:**
```
| C  B  | A  Ab | G  Db | C     |
```

**Gospel Style:**
```
| C  C/B | Am  Am/G | F  C/E | Dm  G  | C |
```

## 7.5 Improvising Over Tizita Changes

### Strategy 1: Stay Pentatonic, Add Chromatic Approaches

Play the C major pentatonic but add half-step approach notes:

```
Instead of:    C - D - E - G - A
Play:          B-C - C#-D - D#-E - F#-G - G#-A
```

### Strategy 2: Target Chord Tones

Over each chord, aim for its chord tones:

```
| Cmaj7 | - target C, E, G, B
| Am7   | - target A, C, E, G
| G7    | - target G, B, D, F
```

### Strategy 3: Use the "Parent" Major Scale

C major pentatonic is part of C major scale. Use the full scale over basic changes, the pentatonic for emphasis:

```
Play: C - D - E - F - G - A - B
Emphasize: C - D - E - G - A (Tizita tones)
```

### Strategy 4: Modal Borrowing

Borrow notes from Tizita Minor for color:

```
Major Tizita: C - D - E - G - A
Minor Tizita: C - D - Eb - G - Ab

Use Eb and Ab as passing tones for bluesy/minor color.
```

## 7.6 Ethiopian Ornamentation

### Vibrato Styles

Ethiopian vibrato is often wider and slower than Western. Practice:
- Slow, exaggerated vibrato on held notes
- Covering nearly a half-step in the wobble

### Grace Note Ornaments

Quick slides and hammer-ons before main notes:

```
          sl.      h
e|----/2--2----0h2--2---|
```

### Trills and Turns

Quick alternation between a note and its neighbor:

```
     tr~~~~~
e|---2~~~~~~~---------|
```

### Quarter-Tone Bends

Ethiopian music often uses microtones. On guitar, approximate with small bends:

```
   1/4
e|--2b-----------|  (Bend up slightly, less than half step)
```

# PART 8: SONG ANALYSIS AND CONCEPTS {#part-8-song-analysis}

## 8.1 Analyzing Neo-Soul Harmony

*Note: These are harmonic concepts and chord progression patterns observed in the genre. Study these patterns and apply them to your own compositions.*

### The IVmaj7 - iii7 - vi7 Pattern

A common neo-soul pattern is this stepwise descent:

```
| Fmaj7 | Em7 | Am7 |

In Roman numerals (key of C): | IVmaj7 | iii7 | vi7 |
```

This creates a smooth descending bass line: F - E - A (with the jump to A creating resolution).

### The I - V/vi - vi - IV Pattern

```
| C | E7 | Am | F |

The E7 is the secondary dominant of Am, creating tension before the minor chord.
```

### The Chromatic Walkdown Pattern

```
| I | I/7 | vi | vi/b6 | IV | iv | I |

In C: | C | C/B | Am | Am/Ab | F | Fm | C |

Or with full chords:
| Cmaj7 | Bm7 | Am7 | Abmaj7 | Fmaj7 | Fm7 | Cmaj7 |
```

### The Gospel Turnaround Pattern

```
| I | I | IV | #IVdim7 | I/V | V7 | I |

In C: | C | C | F | F#dim7 | C/G | G7 | C |
```

### The Neo-Soul ii-V Pattern

Instead of resolving ii-V-I directly, neo-soul often:
- Delays the resolution
- Substitutes the V chord
- Chains multiple ii-Vs

```
| Dm7 | Db7 | Cmaj7 |  (tritone sub for G7)
| Dm7 | Fm7 | Cmaj7 |  (substitute with iv minor)
| Dm7 | G7#9 | Cmaj7 |  (altered dominant)
```

## 8.2 Common Neo-Soul Chord Movements

### The bVII Chord

Using the flat-seven chord from the parallel minor:

```
In C major: Bb or Bbmaj7

| Cmaj7 | Bbmaj7 | Am7 | G7 | Cmaj7 |
```

### The bVI Chord

Another borrowed chord:

```
In C major: Ab or Abmaj7

| Cmaj7 | Abmaj7 | G7 | Cmaj7 |
```

### The iv Minor Chord

Borrowing the minor four chord:

```
| Cmaj7 | Fmaj7 | Fm7 | Cmaj7 |

The shift from F major to F minor creates poignant emotion.
```

### The #IV dim7 (or #IV m7b5)

```
| Cmaj7 | Fmaj7 | F#dim7 | Cmaj7/G |

The F#dim7 creates tension that resolves down to C/G.
```

## 8.3 Rhythm and Feel

### Behind the Beat

Neo-soul sits slightly behind the beat, creating a relaxed, laid-back feel.

**Practice:** Set a metronome and consciously play your chord changes just after the click.

### Syncopation

Emphasize the "and" of beats:

```
Count: 1  +  2  +  3  +  4  +
Play:     x     x     x     x  (play on the ands)
```

### Rhythmic Subdivision

Neo-soul often uses 16th note subdivisions with ghost notes:

```
Count: 1 e + a 2 e + a 3 e + a 4 e + a
Play:  x . x . . x . x . . x x . . x .
```

## 8.4 Tone and Touch

### Recommended Amp Settings

- **Clean channel** with slight breakup
- **Bass:** 5-6
- **Mids:** 6-7
- **Treble:** 4-5
- **Presence:** 3-4

### Effects

1. **Compression** - Light to moderate, for sustain and evenness
2. **Chorus** - Subtle, slow rate, low depth
3. **Reverb** - Plate or spring, moderate decay
4. **Delay** - Short slapback or quarter-note delay, low mix

### Touch

- Play with the **fingers** or a **thin pick**
- Roll off the **tone knob** slightly for warmth
- Use the **neck pickup** for chords, bridge for leads

# PART 10: BUILDING YOUR OWN VOCABULARY {#part-10-vocabulary}

## 10.1 Creating Personal Licks

### The Lick Construction Formula

1. **Start with a chord tone** - root, 3rd, 5th, or 7th
2. **Move through the scale** - by step or skip
3. **Add chromatic approach** - half-step into target
4. **End on a chord tone** - resolve on a strong beat

### Example: Building a Cmaj7 Lick

```
1. Start on E (3rd of Cmaj7)
2. Move through scale: E - D - C
3. Add chromatic approach: D# before E
4. End on C (root) on beat 1

Result:
e|-----3--2--0--------|
B|--4-----------3--1--|
G|--------------------0|  (0 = C on beat 1)
```

### Personal Lick Development Exercise

Take any lick from this guide and:
1. Play it in all 12 keys
2. Change the rhythm
3. Start on a different chord tone
4. Add more chromatic approaches
5. Simplify or complicate it

## 10.2 Chord Voicing Creation

### The "Drop" Technique

Take any closed voicing and "drop" a voice down an octave:

```
Cmaj7 closed: G-B-C-E (low to high)
Drop 2:       B-G-C-E (drop the second voice from top)
Drop 3:       C-B-G-E (drop the third voice from top)
```

### The "Add" Technique

Take a simple chord and add extensions:

```
C triad:     C-E-G
Add 9:       C-E-G-D
Add 6:       C-E-G-A
Add 9 + 6:   C-E-G-A-D (C6/9)
```

### The "Substitute" Technique

Replace one note with another:

```
Cmaj7:       C-E-G-B
Sus2:        C-D-G-B (replace E with D)
Sus4:        C-F-G-B (replace E with F)
```

## 10.3 Progression Writing

### The Diatonic Foundation

Start with diatonic chords, then substitute:

```
Diatonic: | Cmaj7 | Dm7 | Em7 | Fmaj7 | G7 | Am7 | Bm7b5 | Cmaj7 |
```

### Substitution Options

For each chord, you can substitute:

| Original | Substitutes |
|----------|-------------|
| Cmaj7 | C6/9, Em7, Am7 |
| Dm7 | Dm9, Fmaj7, Bm7b5 |
| Em7 | Em9, G6 |
| Fmaj7 | Dm7, F6/9 |
| G7 | G9, Dm7, Db7 (tritone sub) |
| Am7 | Am9, Cmaj7 |

### Creating Tension and Release

**Tension chords:**
- Dominant 7ths (especially altered: 7#9, 7b9)
- Diminished 7ths
- Tritone subs
- Chromatic approach chords

**Release chords:**
- Major 7ths
- 6/9 chords
- Suspended chords resolving to major

## 10.4 Developing Your Sound

### Listen Actively

When listening to music:
1. Identify the chord types used
2. Notice passing chord moments
3. Focus on the guitar voicings
4. Study the rhythm patterns

### Transcribe Regularly

- Start with simple progressions
- Work up to complex arrangements
- Write down what you learn
- Create a personal "vocabulary book"

### Record Yourself

- Record improvisation sessions
- Listen back critically
- Note what works and what doesn't
- Extract your best ideas as licks

### Combine Influences

Your unique sound comes from combining:
- Traditional techniques (Ethiopian Tizita)
- Modern harmony (neo-soul passing chords)
- Personal expression (your own licks and phrases)

# APPENDIX D: FINAL THOUGHTS

## The Journey

Learning neo-soul guitar and applying it to Ethiopian music is a long-term journey. Remember:

1. **Master the basics first** - solid pentatonic and chord knowledge
2. **Add passing chords gradually** - one type at a time
3. **Practice with intention** - know what you're working on
4. **Listen constantly** - let the music guide you
5. **Be patient** - deep musicality takes years to develop

## The Tizita Connection

Major Tizita and neo-soul share a deep connection:
- Both emphasize the pentatonic framework
- Both use extended harmonies for emotional expression
- Both value feel and groove over technical display
- Both are rooted in oral/aural tradition

By combining these traditions, you create something unique - a bridge between Ethiopian heritage and contemporary expression.

## Keep Growing

This guide is a starting point. Continue to:
- Transcribe music you love
- Write your own progressions
- Record and share your music
- Play with other musicians
- Study theory but lead with your ears
